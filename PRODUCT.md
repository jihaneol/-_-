# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are portfolio reviewers and engineers evaluating a commerce/payment system through working admin and shop interfaces. Secondary users are operators who need to inspect members, products, inventory, orders, payments, refunds, and coupon state without guessing where each action lives. Customer-facing shop users need a narrow demo flow: sign up, browse products, buy, and see coupon progress.

## Product Purpose

Card Service demonstrates a transaction-heavy commerce/payment workflow with clear runtime boundaries. The frontend exists to prove the backend capabilities through usable screens: admin surfaces expose operational control and inspection, while shop surfaces expose customer-safe purchase and coupon flows.

## Brand Personality

Clear, controlled, trustworthy.

The interface should feel like an internal operations tool with enough polish to be portfolio-ready. It should not feel like a marketing landing page, a playful consumer app, or a decorative SaaS template.

## Anti-references

- Marketing-style hero pages that hide the actual workflow.
- Purple/blue gradient SaaS dashboards.
- Card-heavy pages where every repeated item has the same decorative treatment.
- Decorative motion, glass effects, oversized rounded panels, and generic AI dashboard styling.
- Customer screens that expose admin operations or operational vocabulary.

## Design Principles

- Keep the task visible: every screen should make the next operational or customer action obvious.
- Separate authority: admin and shop must look related but never interchangeable.
- Prefer dense clarity over decoration: tables, forms, and state badges should scan quickly.
- Use color as status and action language, not background ornament.
- Preserve confidence: errors, loading, empty states, and disabled states must be explicit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support keyboard focus on all interactive elements. Avoid motion that delays the task, and keep any transitions short with a reduced-motion fallback.
